For USC Keck Medical Students

Resources organized around what you’re actually doing in class: ICM, systems blocks, clinical skills, and early patient encounters.

Aligned with ICM & core courses

This hub is designed to sit alongside your English curriculum, not compete with it. The vision is to match Medical Spanish resources to:

  • ICM history-taking & physical exam sessions
  • Systems-based blocks (e.g., cardio, derm, OB/GYN)
  • Early patient encounters and community clinics

As the project grows, sections can be added for each block: “This week in ICM: try these 5 Spanish phrases with patients.”

Why Medical Spanish matters at Keck

Keck students train in a region with a large Spanish-speaking population. Language-concordant care:

  • Improves trust and adherence
  • Reduces errors when interpreters are unavailable
  • Honors patients’ dignity and lived experiences

This site exists to help you move from “I wish I knew more Spanish” to “I have concrete phrases, scripts, and practice tools to actually use.”

Self-Study Paths

Whether you’re brand-new to Spanish or already conversational, start with a simple track and build from there.

Track A: “I know almost no Spanish”

Focus: survival phrases and high-yield clinical language you can actually use during pre-clinical years.

  • Week 1–2: Greetings, introductions, explaining your role
  • Week 3–4: Chief complaint questions (pain, fever, duration)
  • Week 5–6: Basic review of systems & social history questions

Suggested tools:

  • Printable phrase sheets (PDF) for each week
  • Short audio clips to mimic and repeat (1–3 minutes each)
  • Simple Quizlet / Anki decks focused on phrases, not rules

Track B: “I speak some Spanish already”

Focus: tightening medical vocabulary, sounding more natural, and building exam-specific scripts.

  • Refine patient interview scripts for common complaints
  • Specialty mini-modules (OB/GYN, derm, anemia, etc.)
  • Practice with standardized “mini-conversations” (SP style)

Suggested tools:

  • Case-based dialogues to read or role-play
  • Listening practice with podcasts / YouTube (curated links)
  • Advanced phrase banks for nuance and empathy
